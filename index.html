Intro: The Psychology Behind The Purr

On the surface, Cats seem simple and humble creatures, although their minds reveal so much more. Cats have an outstanding memory that they hold, are great problem solvers, and might seem quiet, although they understand various human gestures. In the following research, we will be able to understand the impressive minds of cats and why their behavior and intellectual thinking are much deeper than they may seem.

Voices, Memory, and Feline Minds

Cats are far more capable of understanding their sounds and surroundings than we interpret. Scientific research shows that cats can find their owners' location solely based on their voice, regardless of seeing them. For instance, when a cat suddenly hears a recognizable voice, they often show signs of being surprised, interested, or frightened, which means they are interpreting where their owner is located, technically visualizing a “mental map”. Therefore, this ability goes so far as to prove that cats are intelligent animals that are actively processing information

Cats' Condition After The Loss of Individuals

Many scientific researchers have proved how many cats mourn the losses of other pets and lives. As they express their mourning by having less appetite, being less interested in playing, being isolated from others, and even seeking attention. This suggests that cats not only feel physically but can also experience emotional impacts; therefore, cats can form emotional bonds and connections with other animals and humans, and not just instinctive relationships.

A Cat's Ability to Form Bonds with Humans and Other Animals

Scientists ran tests similar to what they would use on infants and used them on cats, and it revealed that cats, in fact, form a true connection and secure bond with their caregiver, just as dogs and children. This proves how cats use humans as a source of comfort and truly honor and care about their caregivers.

How Cats Behave and Interestingly View Humans

Cats strangely but interestingly don't view us a total different species, although think us as ordinary cats as they treat humans with the same ways as other cats such as, rubbing against you, obtaining your scent, slowly blink at you, focus on you and many more various actions as if we intellectually think about it these are the exact same social behavior cats do to other cats.
